# pilot.md — The GTM-Systems Curriculum Pilot

## What it is
The first real run of the M2W core: build a GTM-systems curriculum deliverable from located raw
material. The domain is **GTM systems engineering**, treated as **civil-engineering-shaped** —
assembling proven components into load-bearing infrastructure others run on — **not**
AI-systems / software-engineering-shaped, even though the vocabulary overlaps. That distinction is a
property of THIS domain and lives here, not in the core seam definition.

## Why this domain was chosen
- **Reversible.** No live leads, nothing irreversible. A wrong call costs a thrown-out dig, not a
  burned lead. This is what makes it safe to TRAIN and GRADE the seam here before live work.
- **Has a clean negative space.** Some source material (an AI-engineering curriculum fed in as if it
  were GTM) does NOT fit. The model is kept **blind** to which material is off-seam, forcing it to
  discover the negative space by reasoning rather than confirming a told answer.

## The reversibility asterisk (precisely)
- The **deliverable** (the course) is reversible — throw it away, no cost.
- The **learning** is **sticky-but-inspectable** — it persists past this pilot. The between-sub-run
  AUDITS in `dry-run.md` are REQUIRED, not optional, because bad training carries forward into live
  work. "Reversible" means the DIG is throwable; it does NOT mean nothing is at stake. The seam's
  training QUALITY is at stake, and the audits protect it.

## What this pilot produces (for the core)
- A trained, inspectable seam layer (the OUTPUT of the run, not an input) → `learned-seam/`.
- The first deliverable, which doubles as a test of conformance-by-shape and the done-gate.
- Evidence for the questions the core defers to a run (see `dry-run.md` BLOCKED-ON-RUN).

## What this pilot is NOT
- It is not the core. Nothing here may be referenced by a core file.
- It is not live GTM. Operational/live-lead GTM is a later, irreversible phase, gated on this pilot
  passing its blind-discovery grade. Until then it stays out of scope (tailed off-seam).
