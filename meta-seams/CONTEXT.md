# CONTEXT.md — meta-seams/

**Meta-seams** are cross-pilot standards on *how* a deliverable is made — distinct from a pilot's
**seam**, which sorts *what material* belongs to the one system under build.

- A **seam** answers *"is this material on-seam for my system?"* — per-pilot, sorts material at the assay.
- A **meta-seam** answers *"does the output meet this cross-cutting standard?"* — every pilot, shapes output.

**Meta-seams are auto-applied, not opt-in.** A cold agent applies one whenever its trigger is present,
in every pilot — without being told. They're distilled *rules*, not reference dumps.

Current meta-seams:
- **`writing.md`** — clarity + persuasion rules, paraphrased from *On Writing Well* (Zinsser) and the
  *Cult of Copy* almanac (Theriot). **Trigger: any deliverable that involves writing or persuasion →
  apply it automatically.** It is the writing bar every prose output the engine produces must clear.
